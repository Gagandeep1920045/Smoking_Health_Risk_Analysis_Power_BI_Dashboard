# Smoking_Health_Risk_Analysis_Power_BI_Dashboard

## 1.	Project Title / Headline  
#### Smoking Health Risk Analysis📈
Empowering Clinical Decisions through Patient Data Visualization

## 2. Short Description / Purpose  
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of the physiological impacts of smoking across a patient demographic of 155 individuals. By correlating smoking status with biometric data like BMI, age, and cholesterol, the dashboard identifies high-risk cohorts to assist healthcare providers in targeted intervention and preventative care.

## 3. Tech Stack
The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:  
•	📊 Power BI Desktop – Main data visualization platform used for report creation.  
•	📂 Power Query – Data transformation and cleaning layer for reshaping and preparing the data.  
•	🧠 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Used for calculated measures, dynamic visuals, and conditional logic.  
•	📝 Data Modeling – Relationships established among tables (Health dataset , condition, organs) to  
enable cross-filtering and aggregation.  
•	📁 File Format – .pbix for development and .png for dashboard previews.  

## 4. Features / Highlights  
### 📌 Business Problem  
Healthcare providers often struggle to visualize the multi-organ impact of lifestyle choices in a single view. Identifying which demographic (age/gender) is most susceptible to secondary risks like hypertension and high cholesterol due to smoking is critical for reducing long-term treatment costs.

### 📌 Goal of the Dashboard  
The primary objective is to evaluate the correlation between smoking habits (duration/intensity) and systemic health risks (BMI, Hypertension, Cholesterol) to identify the most "at-risk" patient segments.

### 📌 Walkthrough of Key Visuals  
* Executive Summary KPI: Displays total patient count (155) against critical averages like Age ($57.5$) and BMI ($29.2$).
* Smoking Status Distribution: A donut chart showing that over 50% of the demographic consists of "Never" smokers, providing a strong baseline for comparison against "Current" and "Former" groups.
* Sankey-style Demographic Flow: Visualizes "Smoking Status by Gender," highlighting how habits differ between male and female cohorts.
* Risk Correlation Matrix: A stacked bar chart analyzing "Cholesterol & Hypertension Risk" across age groups, showing a clear trend of "High" risk levels in older demographics.
* Interactive Anatomy Map: Navigation buttons (Heart, Kidney, Liver, Lungs) suggest a drill-through capability to explore organ-specific health impacts.

### 📌 Business Impact & Insights
* High BMI Correlation: The average BMI of $29.2$ indicates a population leaning toward the overweight/obese category, which may compound the cardiovascular risks of smoking.
* Age-Risk Escalation: Insights reveal that the $59$-$68$ age bracket shows significant hypertension and cholesterol risks, suggesting this group requires the most immediate clinical outreach.
* Intake Trends: The line chart for "Daily Intake" (CPD) vs. "Years of Smoking" (YOS) identifies the intensity of habits, allowing for personalized smoking cessation plans.
